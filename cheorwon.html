Cheorwon: Heaven for Cranes

Published on Jan 14, 2026 by Andy

Cheorwon represents one of the most significant wintering grounds for cranes in East Asia. Located near the DMZ, this area provides a pristine habitat for these majestic birds.

Why Cheorwon?

Every winter, thousands of White-naped Cranes and Red-crowned Cranes migrate to this area. The vast rice paddies provide ample food, while the restrictions of the civilian control line offer them safety.

Originally inside the DMZ, the control line has retreated North over time, allowing civilians to access more areas for crane ecotours.

Planning Your Visit

  • Best Time: December to February. Early morning is magical.
  • Transport: Car is necessary.
  • ID Required: Passport is mandatory for foreigners (military checkpoints & DMZ tours).

Birding Tips & Ethics

  • Stay in your Car: Do not exit the vehicle near raptors or cranes. They will be flushed easily. Your car is the best blind.
  • Drive Carefully: Roads between rice fields are narrow. Vegetation can scratch your car, so proceed with caution.
  • Keep Distance: Cranes are shy. Do not drive too close.
