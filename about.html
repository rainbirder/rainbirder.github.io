Hi, I'm Rainbirder

Your Guide to Korean Avifauna

Hello! My name is Andy Lee, also known as Rainbirder in the Korean birding community. I am a birder based in the Republic of Korea.

I created this website with a single goal: to introduce birding in Korea to foreigners who are new to the country or travelers visiting this peninsula.

Why Bird in Korea?

You might be surprised to learn that this small peninsula is a powerhouse for bird biodiversity. Around 600 species have been recorded here.

Korea serves as a critical stopover and wintering ground on the East Asian-Australasian Flyway (EAAF). Our diverse habitats attract thousands of migratory birds annually, along with spectacular wintering waterfowls such as ducks, geese, and endangered cranes.

Bridging the Gap

For many non-Korean speakers, accessing information about birding here has been difficult. Much of the local birding information (especially recent ones) has been unintentionally "gatekept" simply because there are no large English-speaking birding communities in Korea.

This website aims to break down that barrier by providing accessible guides to prime birding spots and detailed information on target species.
